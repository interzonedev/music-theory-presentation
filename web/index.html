Music Theory

General overview of the basics of sound and music theory

What is Sound?

Sound is the movement of pressure changes in a material like air or metal.

What is Pressure?

Pressure is how "hard" air is pressing against a surface.

We feel the pressure of the air around us on our bodies. It feels normal but if we go to higher altitude, we can feel the pressure decrease in our ears.

When air is "squeezed" into a smaller volume, the pressure gets higher.

For example, the air pressure inside a balloon is greater than the air pressure outside the balloon.

Vibration

Repetitive back and forth movement.

Can be a pendulum, a tuning fork, a spring, a guitar string, etc.

Also known as oscillation.

When a object vibrates, it causes similar vibrations in the pressure of the surrounding air. These vibrations are detected by the human ear and processed by the brain as sound.

This is called a sound wave.

The distance between the high pressure areas of the sound wave is called the wavelength.
